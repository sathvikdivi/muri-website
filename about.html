Key objectives of this initiative are as follows:

  • Define and fabricate the units of impulsive propulsion.
  • Resolve the scaling limits and efficiency of energy storage in biological and engineered systems
  • Define the material, mechanical and structural basis for the inherent asymmetry in energy flow of impulsive systems
  • Test the role of environment-system interactions in energy conversion, scaling and dissipation to achieve enhanced repeatability

Key quiding questions:

  • Under which conditions should a system be driven by a motor versus a spring?
  • How does spring force-velocity behavior yield power amplification?
  • How do latches influence the dynamics of energy release?
  • What are the principles of integrated tuning and how should they be applied to analysis of biological system performance and synthesis of repeatable, efficient devices?
